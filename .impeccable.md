## Design Context

This file is the source of truth for design decisions across Innovate App Studios. It guides every `/impeccable` invocation and should be consulted before any design work. It pairs with — and does not replace — `DESIGN.md` (the Lamborghini aesthetic reference) and `CLAUDE.md` (engineering conventions).

The project has two distinct surfaces that share a palette and a brand voice but diverge in density, rhythm, and intent:

1. **Marketing site** (`app/(marketing)/`) — the outward face. Theatrical, confident, spacious.
2. **Internal CRM** (`app/(protected)/`) — the working tool. Dense, utilitarian, data-forward.

Treat them as two dialects of one design system. The atoms are shared (primary blue, zero radius, Geist Mono headings, 4px grid, shadcn primitives). The sentences are different.

---

### Users

**Marketing site audience.** Small-to-mid business owners evaluating a web, mobile, SaaS, or AI partner. They arrive skeptical — they've been burned by template agencies, quoted hourly by consultancies, or outpriced by enterprise shops. They scan before they read. They need to feel, within 5 seconds, that this studio is real, capable, and priced honestly. Mobile traffic is significant; many will land from a phone on Instagram or WhatsApp.

**CRM audience.** A small internal sales team plus admin (currently you, with `sales_rep`, `editor`, `admin` roles defined). Power users who will use this every day. They need to move fast through clients, leads, projects, and commission math — keyboard-friendly, information-dense, low-chrome. No customer ever logs in here; this is not a client portal.

**Job-to-be-done per surface.**
- Marketing: *"Can this studio actually build what I need, and can I afford it without getting trapped?"*
- CRM: *"Where's that lead, what stage is that project in, what's my pipeline worth, what's my cut?"*

---

### Brand Personality

Three words: **trustworthy, modern, premium.**

Not luxury-premium in the exclusive-club sense. Premium in the *"the craftsmanship is obvious, the pricing is clear, nothing here is sloppy"* sense. Closer to a well-made mechanical tool than to a velvet rope.

**Voice traits that follow:**
- Direct. No marketing fluff, no "transformative synergies." The copy in `lib/site.ts` sets the tone: "20 min ▪ Free ▪ No pitch." "Fixed price, no hourly." "Cancel anytime." Match that everywhere.
- Confident without shouting. The Lamborghini reference is about *discipline and precision*, not aggression. Uppercase display type is a structural convention, not a raised voice.
- Tactile. Sharp edges, real measurements, concrete numbers. Avoid vague superlatives ("amazing results") — use specifics ("5–7 days", "$97/mo", "12 months").
- Unafraid of silence. Large negative space (dark, in this system) is part of the voice.

**Emotional promise.** Within 5 seconds of landing, the visitor should feel: *"these people know what they're doing, they're not hiding the price, and they're not going to waste my time."*

### Aesthetic Direction

**The canonical canvas is dark.** `app/layout.tsx` sets `defaultTheme="dark"`, and the design system is built around absolute black with a single blue primary and generous negative space. Light mode exists as a sensible inversion for accessibility and print, not as a parallel design track — don't spend equal creative effort on it.

**Palette discipline.** The system is monochrome-plus-one:
- Absolute black background, charcoal (`--card`) for elevated surfaces, borders at ~12% white.
- Primary blue (`--primary` = `#0099FF` dark / `#0173E6` light) reserved for *actions only* — buttons, links, focus rings, active states. Never on eyebrows, numerals, bullets, or plain text. This is a hard rule already codified in memory; it applies universally.
- Status palettes (stage, lead status, payment status) live in `lib/status-colors.ts` — use those helpers (`projectStageTone()`, `leadStatusTone()`, `paymentStatusBadgeClass()`) rather than redefining color semantics per screen.
- Gray text on colored backgrounds is banned; tint neutrals toward the background, not toward neutral gray.

**Typography.** Geist Mono for headings (`--font-heading`) and Noto Sans for body (`--font-sans`). The Geist Mono headings are the project's signature — they evoke the Lamborghini "LamboType" spirit (geometric, precise, technical) without leaning on the actual font. Do not swap either font. When composing new screens, use Geist Mono for display / section titles / numerals-as-display, and Noto Sans for all reading text, form labels, and table content. Minimum font size is `text-xs`; never use arbitrary sizes like `text-[10px]`.

**Geometry.** `--radius: 0` is deliberate and non-negotiable. No rounded cards, no rounded buttons, no pill shapes except where the design system already allows them (e.g. the shadcn switch, badges at radius 2px). Corners are sharp. Borders are 1px. Dividers are hairline.

**Spacing.** 4px grid, always. Prefer Tailwind defaults (`gap-2`, `gap-3`, `gap-4`, `p-6`, `p-8`) over arbitrary `[Npx]` values. Use `gap` on flex/grid containers rather than margin chains. Vary spacing across a screen to create rhythm — don't pad every container with the same number.

**Components.** shadcn-only. Never hand-roll a div that mimics Card/Button/Select/Input. Use the existing primitives in `components/ui/*`. For inputs with prefixes, suffixes, units, or inline buttons, default to `InputGroup` rather than bare `Input`. For selects, always wrap items in `SelectGroup` with a `SelectLabel` header, and use the render-prop form of `SelectValue` with a `LABEL_MAP` so enum values never leak into the UI (e.g. never show "active_client" — show "Active client").

**Language conventions.** These are shipped product decisions, not stylistic preferences:
- "Rep" (not "Owner") for the assigned profile in all user-facing labels. `OwnerChip` is the component; the label says Rep.
- "Business" (not "Company") for `clients.company` in all UI labels. The DB column name stays.
- Clients never get avatars or initials tiles. Rep avatars via `OwnerChip` are fine.
- In the leads table, the person's name leads; in the clients table, the business name leads. Don't align them — they serve different mental models.

**Anti-references.** Explicitly do NOT design toward:
- *Webflow template agency sites.* Giant hero, rotating client logos, a card grid of services with icon + heading + body, gradient blobs behind the headline, a testimonial carousel, a final "let's build something amazing together" CTA. This template is the single thing we are defining ourselves against.
- *Squarespace-style small-business sites.* Soft rounded corners, pastel palettes, photographic hero with a white overlay, identical three-card feature rows, generic serif pairings.
- *Y Combinator startup aesthetic.* Inter + orange, "we're a team of ex-[FAANG] engineers", flat illustration, gradient CTA buttons.
- *AI-generated marketing aesthetic.* Cyan-on-dark, purple-to-blue gradients, glowing accents, floating glass cards, border-left colored stripes, gradient text. All of these are banned in the core rules; calling them out explicitly here because every generic agency site converges on them.

**What we want to look like instead.** A discipline that borrows the *structural confidence* of Lamborghini's marketing (true black, uppercase display, sharp corners, single accent), the *data clarity* of Linear/Attio/Vercel's dashboards, and the *editorial restraint* of a well-set technical manual. Premium not because it's decorated — premium because every element is intentional and nothing is wasted.

### Surface-Specific Direction

**Marketing site — theatrical restraint.**
- Generous vertical space between sections. Darkness IS the whitespace; use it.
- Large display headings in Geist Mono, uppercase, tight line-height (0.95–1.05).
- Asymmetric layouts. Break the grid intentionally for emphasis. Don't center everything.
- One primary CTA per section at most. Ghost / text-link variants for secondary actions.
- Numbers (process steps `01`–`05`, pricing figures, timelines) are a design element — show them large, in Geist Mono, but never in primary color.
- Full-bleed moments are welcome (hero, product showcase). Cards are a last resort, not a default.
- Mobile scales down the display type aggressively but keeps the rhythm — don't collapse into a generic responsive stack.

**CRM — utility, density, data-clarity.**
- Closer in spirit to Linear, Attio, or Vercel's dashboard than to the marketing site. Same palette, different pacing.
- Information density is a feature. Tables should feel like tables — compact rows, clear column alignment, scannable. Don't pad rows into cards.
- Keyboard affordances matter: visible focus states (primary blue ring), logical tab order, `cmd+k`-style patterns where they fit.
- Status communication via `lib/status-colors.ts` — consistent tones across stage, lead status, payment status.
- Sidebars, detail panels, and drawers over modals. Modals are a last resort (already codified).
- Empty states teach the workflow, not just say "nothing here" (e.g. "No leads yet. Add one from the top-right, or import a CSV.").
- Avoid the hero-metric template (one big number + label + sparkline). Real analytics need real charts; decorative metrics should be cut.
- Progressive disclosure: surface the 80% case, tuck the rest behind "Advanced" or a secondary panel.

### Design Principles

When in doubt, these five principles arbitrate. They are ranked — earlier principles win ties.

1. **Intentional, not decorated.** Every element justifies its existence. If a divider, icon, or stripe doesn't carry information or structural load, remove it. Decoration-for-decoration's-sake is the AI-slop tell we most want to avoid.

2. **One voice, two dialects.** Marketing and CRM share atoms (palette, fonts, 4px grid, zero radius, shadcn) but differ in rhythm (marketing is spacious and theatrical, CRM is dense and utilitarian). When building a component, ask which surface it belongs to — and let that decide padding, type scale, and information density.

3. **Primary color is a spotlight, not paint.** Primary blue belongs on actions and focus. If it's touching plain text, eyebrows, numerals, bullets, or backgrounds, it's wrong. Status lives in `lib/status-colors.ts`; brand lives in `--primary`. They do not mix.

4. **Sharp over soft.** Zero radius, 1px borders, hairline dividers, tight line-heights on display type. Softness (rounded corners, drop shadows, blurred glass) is what Squarespace and Webflow templates do; we do the opposite by default.

5. **Honesty is premium.** Concrete numbers ($97/mo, 5–7 days, 30%), plain verbs ("We deploy" not "We facilitate deployment"), visible tradeoffs. The brand's premium feel comes from refusing to hide things, not from adding visual polish.
